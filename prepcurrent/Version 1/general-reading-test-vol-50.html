ROMANCE IN THE WORK PLACE
  1. Romantic relationships in the work place has always been a touchy subject. With people on either side of the divide equally against and for them, it has become nigh impossible to voice your opinion on the subject without getting attacked by a die-hard member of either the “for” or “against” group depending on your stance.
  2. But why is it such a controversial subject? The average employee spends about 10 hours of their day in the office out of the 24 in the day. If you deduct sleeping time, approximately 8 hours, preparation for and commuting to and from work, another 3 – 4 hours depending on the city you live in and traffic congestion, that leaves between 2 – 3 hours of time free to socialize and bond with others outside your work environment on weekdays.
  3. For those who work weekends as well, it becomes much tougher to meet and cultivate meaning relationships outside the work place. Recent research places the percentage of people who meet their romantic partners at work at 22 compared to the 13, 18 and 10 percent that meet theirs online, through friends and on a night out respectively.
  4. So why are so many people still against this obvious frequently occurring circumstance? Firstly, there is the argument for focus. We all know that with romantic entanglements, at least for those in their early stages, productivity is at an all-time low. People involved in the early stages of a love relationship are affected by the cocktail of released neurochemicals that causes them to experience euphoria similar to that caused by drugs. This means that involved individuals are not able to focus on their given tasks and the job suffers.
  5. Secondly, there are the associated issues with favoritism where the involved persons work in close proximity with each other, or the contrasting issues of victimization by disapproving colleagues. It is no wonder then that many couples who find each other in the work tend to keep their relationship secret to avoid the scrutiny and backlash that comes with the territory.
  6. Even with all the possible hurdles and stigma faced by those in this situation, it is obvious that work place romances are not going anywhere anytime soon because as the saying goes, “you cannot choose when or where you fall in love”.

The advent of social media has made the world smaller. With people making connections with strangers across the world and sharing moments of their lives, friendships and even romantic relationships are built online.

While social media undeniably has its pro’s, from making the world more aware of goings on in different countries like the genocides and crimes previously suppressed by governments or overlooked by the media, to aiding businesses to reach a wider audience, to creating the next generation of superstars and influencers, it has become a growing concern that the con’s may outweigh the pro’s.

In an age where privacy is fast becoming an outdated notion, the dangers of the sheer number of people that we open our lives to on social sites is unquantifiable. Firstly, the internet offers anonymity to users. You may be chatting with a person who claims to be one thing but is something else entirely. Numerous cases of what is now referred to as cat-fishing have surfaced where people think they have been communicating with someone entirely different from who they in reality have been communicating with.

As innocent as it sounds, this has led to a number of scams on mostly older women seeking love. Their vulnerability and trusting nature, no doubt a remnant of a time when people were who they said they were and verification happened instantaneously, have made them prime targets for these love scams.

Some victims have lost hundreds of thousands of dollars with some reportedly losing their entire life savings to scammers under the guise of requiring the funds to facilitate a meeting or as a short term loan in a dire situation.

Cat-fishing is far from being the only danger on social media as a number of violent crimes such as murder have been facilitated by the criminals tracking the victims through social media. Most troubling is the exposure of underage children to predators when they gain access to social media sites. With the only requirement to opening an account being an email address and access to the internet, children as young as 7 years old have been found to have opened social media accounts without the knowledge of their parents.

Reports of children getting propositioned and lured by pedophiles have become rampant and parents have been advised to keep a close eye on their children’s’ online activities. In a bid to ensure their safety and keep them away from social sites they shouldn’t be on, some parents have taken to cloning their children’s phones and covertly monitoring their communications.

So while social media is all the rage with people trying to outshine their peers with flashy displays of wealth, accomplishments, and an interesting life, it is important to remember that a lot of what is shown to the public by users is fake, exaggerated or edited. People are not always who they say they are, and there is danger in overexposure.

The singling out of one group for positive attention above other groups has been severally compared to racism in recent times with the excluded majority feeling attacked or undervalued. With movements such as “Black Lives Matter” and “Me Too”, which address the loss of African American lives to police brutality, and the issues of sexual harassment and violence towards women, counter groups have established a movement to either reduce the impact of the formers or to point out the gaps they create.

In the case of the black lives matter movement which was proliferated with the hashtag trend, members of other racial groups decried exclusion and soon began to chant “All lives matter”. With the “me too” movement, men who felt excluded or persecuted by the message of the group, began their own movement to counter it. The “Him too” movement.

In the case of the former, the counter movement contends that the message passed by the preceding movement was that the only lives that mattered were the black lives, whereas the policemen who were involved in the brutality cases were in fear for their own lives and that their lives matter as well.

With movements like these, it is easy to take sides vehemently without considering the counter argument or fully understanding why a movement was created in the first place. When a group feels wronged or specifically targeted, and start a movement to address that wrong, they are speaking up from their own experiences and views. This should not in any way downplay or invalidate others who have experienced the same things and not voiced out or received the same attention.

By saying blue is a beautiful colour, it does not automatically then mean that only the colour blue is beautiful. It means that at the time of the declaration, the colour blue was foremost on the speaker’s mind and he felt compelled to express his opinion on the matter. Trends and frequent occurrences bring certain issues to the limelight and while the spotlight is focused on them, they stand out more than other matters. As the media is the main source of information for most individuals, what gets repeatedly reported, gains popularity and attention, and expression of one opinion on one matter, does not mean people do not have similar opinions on closely related matters.

Protesters from either side of the divide often end up attacking each other instead of realizing that they are usually halves of a whole that when put together not only solve the issues but create the balance everyone is looking for.

If the messages of the “Back lives matter” and “All lives matter” groups were to be unified, it would become “ black lives matter as much as all other lives, so treat us all the same” and the messages of the “me too” and “ him too” movements would be “ stop sexual violence and harassment against everyone”.

It is important to understand though that when a movement like the “Black Lives Matter” and “Me Too” movements surface, it is in response to victimization of a certain group of people, and their cries for an end to the victimization should not be drowned out by inconsequential arguments that do not address the specific victimization they are decrying.

  1. As important as work place etiquette is, it is sad to see that nowadays many employees do not know the proper conduct for the work place. As such, organizations that should otherwise have a great work atmosphere, are reporting low job satisfaction from their employees. Bosses and colleagues using foul language and raised voices to express their displeasure or disappointment with how others do their jobs, underhanded tactics to discredit colleagues and inappropriate discussions and expressions are only a few of the myriad of issues organizations face.
  2. Office etiquette is the code that guides appropriate workplace social behavior and conduct. Although there is no uniform standard of office etiquette, an ideal etiquette handbook should cover aspects such as hygiene, language, interpersonal relationships, phone etiquette, attire etc.
  3. Etiquette like all other behaviors, evolves with the times but its significance will never wane. It aids us in displaying the acceptable behavior for a given environment and without it, relationships and society will devolve into one without polite manners. When properly utilized, etiquette aids in building relationships and diffusing tempers, and can even be the basis of selection of businesses to patronize.
  4. An example of how profound the impact of good workplace etiquette can be, is the case of a large cake shop equipment supplier in Coventry. The business was the only one of its kind for miles and enjoyed a comfortable monopoly. Customers came from neighboring towns to get the equipment that they needed and because of this, the staff and management developed haughty attitudes and were unfriendly to their customers. As the only choice around, business continued to boom. Around the time that they made a large shipment purchase, a similar business opened up in the neighboring town of Birmingham. Although their prices were higher and customers had to make a longer journey to get to them, they were pleasant and displayed the proper etiquette for operations. In no time at all, the business in Coventry had lost all its customers to the business in Birmingham and had to close down due to rising debt.
  5. For better interpersonal relationships with customers and between colleagues, it is advised that workplace etiquette courses be made mandatory for staff and an etiquette guidebook be made available to them.
