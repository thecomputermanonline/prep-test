William: precisely! Good memory you’ve got there. So, it starts out as a dinner party with slow music and tributes and speeches, then becomes a dance party that lasts till the early hours of the next day.

Jessica: oh? It seems the format has been changed.

William: good catch Jessica. Yes it has slightly. We had initially thought to make it a very formal occasion with the dinner party part but the younger ones on our team seem to think including an after party with dancing in it will make it a more inclusive event and a more memorable one as well.

Jessica: that’s a pretty good argument and it gives everyone a little something to look forward to as well. And if we include music from across the generations, we can ensure it is a party that everyone will enjoy.

William: our thoughts exactly! We are looking to include music from the 70’s through to music of today.

Jessica: that’s perfect! A musical journey that reflects his career years

William: I think you’ve just given us the theme for the after party.

Jessica: well that’s what I’m here for, glad to be of service.

------------------------------- Beginning of part two--------------------------------------------

William: please give us an overview of the cost items we need to be prepared for once again.

Jessica: well with an event of this size, and with the aspects mentioned, you will firstly need to have a venue that can accommodate both the dinner party and the after party in adjoining rooms. I say adjoining rooms because you want to keep the travel between venues to a minimum.

William: okay

Jessica: then of course you will need to be ready to handle costs for feeding, decoration, lighting, sound, music, …

William: ah Jessica I think we may need to set up a live meeting for all this.

William: yes that will be perfect. Justin and Anna will be in touch with you to set up the next meeting and will be your contacts for the entire event planning.

Jessica: that’s fine with me. They can send me an email and I will confirm once I get it. I look forward to working with you all and executing a flawless event.

William: Thanks for your time Jessica and have a splendid week.

Jessica: you too William. Bye.

Linda: Hi, Michael.

Michael: Hi, Linda. How are you?

Linda: I’m Good. And you?

Michael: Not bad.

Linda: So I heard you are from San Francisco.

Michael: That’s right. I was born there and I lived in the city for many years.

Linda: That’s Cool. So what would you say is the most interesting thing about your city?

Michael: Well, there are many wonderful and interest things about San Francisco, but the most interesting thing is probably the beautiful architecture. There are lots of very unique infrastructure and it in fact attracts the fifth- highest number of tourists of any city in the United States.

Linda: architecture you say, interesting. Is there a building you think is a must-see for all tourists?

Michael: In my opinion, the most unique building and a tourist must-see is probably the Trans-America building, which is built like a pyramid. It’s nice.

Linda: Well, that’s really cool.

Michael: yeah, I think so at least.

Linda: What is the most expensive area in San Francisco?

Michael: Well, the city is very, very expensive. The most expensive area is a place called Pacific Heights. It’s on the highest hill in San Francisco and it overlooks the Bay. It’s really quite beautiful.

Linda: Hmm.

Michael: That area by far has the best views of the city.

Linda: Hmm okay, and what is the most beautiful place in San Francisco?

Michael: Well, actually the most beautiful place is outside the city. It’s a place called Muir Woods.

------------------------------- Beginning of part 2--------------------------------------------

Michael: Muir Woods has the tallest trees in the world, and some of these trees are really old. They are the oldest trees in North America with some of them being over 2,000 years old.

Linda: Wow. It sounds like a place I would really love to see, although it also sounds quite expensive. Are there any cheap places to live?

Michael: well yes of course! There are several but the cheapest place to live is a place called Sunset. Sunset is on the west side, and the houses are kind of old and they are quite affordable. But one thing about Sunset is it has a lovely view of the ocean, and you could watch the sunset every night. That’s why it’s called the Sunset area.

Linda: Hmm. So if I wanted to go there, when would be the best time to visit?

Michael: Well, the best time is probably in the fall because that’s when the weather is the nicest. It’s neither too cold, nor too hot. And it doesn’t rain that much.

Linda: So now I have to ask when the worst time to visit would be.

Michael: (chuckle) the worst time or rather times to visit would probably be February and March, because February and March are the coldest and wettest months. So – well, March isn’t that cold but sometimes it’s wet, it rains a lot.

Linda: Wow. That sounds great. I really want to go there.

Michael: Yeah, you should. It’s great place. I can also recommend a few other things you can do while you’re down there and I’m willing to help with reservations and the like.

Linda: Thanks a lot! It looks like I have next fall set for a visit to San Francisco.

Audio script for Questions 20 - 30 Conversation between colleagues at work.

Ronald: Okay. So Angela, I see you eat soup every day at work. Why do you eat soup every day?

Angela: That’s because I love to cook soup.

Ronald: So you make the soup?

Angela: Yes, it’s very easy to make.

Ronald: So you cook it and then you just bring it to work every day?

Angela: Yeah. I just make a lot on Monday and then I bring it to work every day of the week.

Ronald: Oh nice. So how do you make the soup? What’s your secret?

Angela: Well, I like to cook, but I do not like the preparation process, so where I can I buy pre-cut items like meat and chicken. I also buy the other necessary ingredients like rice, usually brown rice and some vegetables. When I’m ready to cook, I chop my ingredients, put them all together in boiling water. After it boils for a while, I add seasoning and voila! It’s ready.

Ronald: Okay. So you say the water boils, so as soon as the water boils that’s when you put in all the ingredients?

Angela: Yes, that’s right.

Ronald: So you don’t put in the ingredients before the water boils.

Angela: No. I guess, it’s just easier for the water to be hot because then the vegetables and the meat cook a little faster.

------------------------------- Beginning of part 2--------------------------------------------

Ronald: So how do you give the soup flavoring?

Angela: I usually add salt and pepper, maybe some garlic. And depending on the type of soup, either maybe some soy sauce or lemon juice.

Ronald: Okay. Do you put in the flavoring after you put in the ingredients or before you put in the ingredients?

Angela: Maybe after but usually, right about all at the same time.

Ronald: Okay.

Angela: So I just put everything in at one time.

Ronald: And then after you cook the soup, do you put the soup in the refrigerator? Or do you let it sit outside?

Angela: I usually eat some right then, and I also put it in containers for the week. But I let it sit in the containers out on the counter for a while for it to cool before I put in the refrigerator.

Ronald: All right. And so, you don’t put it in the refrigerator until it is cool?

Angela: Until it’s about room temperature.

Ronald: Okay, nice. And then how do you heat it up? Do you heat it up in a pot or do you heat it up in the microwave?

Angela: In the microwave. It’s the easiest.

Ronald: Yeah. Nice. So you make enough for five meals?

Angela: Maybe, sometimes. If I think I will get tired of eating it during the week then maybe I’ll just make enough for three or four meals. But if it’s some kind that I think is really delicious and I know I want to eat it every day, then I’ll make a lot.

Ronald: Well, if that happens, when you make the soup, you can make it for six or seven and give me a bowl.

Angela: Okay. I’ll do that next time.

Ronald: Oh great. Thanks.
